About Me

Born and raised in Atlanta, Georgia. When I finished high school in 2006, I moved to China for a year to study martial arts at an international academy dedicated to the pursuit of willpower. I inadvertantly learned quite a bit of Mandarin over the weekends interacting with the locale. When I returned the culture shock hit me like a megaton hammer so I attempted university at University of Tennessee in Chattanooga to no avail. I moved from Chattanooga, Tennessee to nearby Nashville and formed a band. Five years passed by with many successful live shows including becoming a staple in the legendary Nashville underground scene. Right before a game changing show where the other two bands booked got offered tiny desk and late night show appearances, the band split up and I relocated to a yoga ashram to study the internal mysteries. It occurred to me in this time of living with people from other parts of the world that I should travel. Having been successful in learning and applying many yogic techniques I conquered my anxiety of travel and set out into the world, first to Iceland, then Costa Rica, Norway, Mexico, Canada, and finally ending up in Fairfield, Iowa at a meditating college. I graduated from Maharishi University of Management with a degree in Business focusing on management and entrepreneurship in 2015. At that point I relocated to the west coast after converting my van to a camper and lived in wonder land of Mt. Shasta in Northern California. I climbed the mountain, which is quite an experience, and at the peak I felt that I can do anything in the world. Two years of working in Portland and Mt. Hood in Oregon had passed, as well as living in the Bay Area and up in Washington. I helped start a food truck that became very successful and worked in many kitchens learning much about business management and cooking professionally. Finally I returned to the midwest: Fairfield, Iowa.
This is when I found DeltaV Code School in Cedar Rapids, Iowa. I promptly moved and recently completed the 201 course. I will continue through to the 401 and pursue a carreer in software/web development.
